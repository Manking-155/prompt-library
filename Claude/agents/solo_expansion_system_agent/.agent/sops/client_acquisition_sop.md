# SOP: Client Acquisition Process

## Overview

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the systematic process for acquiring new clients for solo developer services, from lead generation to conversion, ensuring consistent and effective client acquisition strategies.

## Prerequisites

- Personal brand established (LinkedIn, website, portfolio)
- Service offerings clearly defined and priced
- CRM system configured (Excel, Google Sheets, or CRM software)
- Marketing materials prepared (portfolio, case studies, proposals)
- Legal templates ready (contracts, NDAs, proposals)
- Communication channels set up (email, LinkedIn, professional networks)

## Process Overview

### Client Acquisition Funnel
```
Client Acquisition Process
├── Lead Generation (Multiple Channels)
├── Lead Qualification (Initial Screening)
├── Discovery & Needs Assessment (Deep Dive)
├── Proposal & Quote (Formal Offer)
├── Negotiation & Agreement (Final Terms)
├── Contract Signing (Official Engagement)
└── Onboarding (Project Kickoff)
```

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Lead Generation

#### 1.1 Content Marketing Lead Generation
**Frequency**: Weekly activities
**Tools**: LinkedIn, Blog, Social Media, GitHub

**Actions**:
1. **Publish Technical Content** (Weekly)
   - Write one blog post about Flutter/AI development
   - Share on LinkedIn with technical insights
   - Include call-to-action for consultation
   - Use relevant hashtags: #FlutterDev #AIIntegration #VietnamTech

2. **Showcase Work on GitHub** (Ongoing)
   - Update GitHub profile with recent projects
   - Create README files with clear descriptions
   - Add Vietnamese language documentation where applicable
   - Tag with relevant technologies

3. **LinkedIn Engagement** (Daily - 30 minutes)
   - Comment on relevant posts from Vietnamese tech companies
   - Share insights about AI integration for businesses
   - Participate in Flutter development discussions
   - Connect with decision-makers at target companies

4. **Create Valuable Resources** (Monthly)
   - Develop free guide on AI integration for SMEs
   - Create video tutorial on mobile app development
   - Design case study of successful project
   - Share on all channels with lead capture forms

#### 1.2 Networking Lead Generation
**Frequency**: Weekly - 2-3 hours
**Tools**: LinkedIn Events, Meetup.com, Professional Networks

**Actions**:
1. **Tech Community Events** (Monthly)
   - Attend Vietnam tech meetups and conferences
   - Speak at local developer events
   - Participate in hackathons as mentor
   - Network with business owners at startup events

2. **Professional Associations** (Quarterly)
   - Join Vietnamese Software Association (VINASA)
   - Attend chamber of commerce events
   - Participate in industry-specific gatherings
   - Connect with business consultants

3. **Online Communities** (Ongoing)
   - Participate in Vietnamese developer forums
   - Contribute to open source projects
   - Answer questions on Stack Overflow
   - Join relevant Telegram/Zalo groups

#### 1.3 Referral Lead Generation
**Frequency**: Ongoing
**Tools**: Email, LinkedIn, Personal Network

**Actions**:
1. **Existing Client Referrals** (Monthly)
   - Ask satisfied clients for referrals
   - Create referral incentive program
   - Share success stories with client's network
   - Offer finder's fee for qualified leads

2. **Professional Network Referrals** (Monthly)
   - Connect with other developers for overflow work
   - Build relationships with digital agencies
   - Network with business consultants
   - Partner with design studios

3. **Community Referrals** (Quarterly)
   - Participate in community service projects
   - Mentor junior developers
   - Speak at local universities
   - Contribute to Vietnamese tech communities

### Phase 2: Lead Qualification

#### 2.1 Initial Lead Qualification
**Response Time**: Within 24 hours of initial contact
**Tools**: CRM, Email Templates, Lead Scoring Criteria

**Qualification Criteria**:
```markdown
## Lead Qualification Scorecard
### Basic Information (Required)
- [ ] Contact person identified
- [ ] Company name and website
- [ ] Email/phone contact confirmed
- [ ] Initial project brief received

### Business Qualification (Score 1-10)
**Budget**: [ ] <$2,000 (1-3), $2,000-10,000 (4-7), >$10,000 (8-10)
**Timeline**: [ ] Not defined (1-3), 3+ months (4-7), Immediate (8-10)
**Decision Maker**: [ ] Unknown (1-3), Identified (4-7), Contacted (8-10)
**Project Type**: [ ] Small enhancement (1-3), New app (4-7), Strategic project (8-10)

### Technical Qualification (Score 1-10)
**Complexity**: [ ] Simple (1-3), Moderate (4-7), Complex (8-10)
**Technology Fit**: [ ] Poor fit (1-3), Good fit (4-7), Perfect fit (8-10)
**Timeline**: [ ] Flexible (1-3), Moderate (4-7), Urgent (8-10)

### Total Score: [ ] (Total of all scores)
```

#### 2.2 Lead Categorization
**Categories**:
- **Hot Leads**: Score 25+ (High priority, immediate follow-up)
- **Warm Leads**: Score 15-24 (Medium priority, schedule call within 3 days)
- **Cool Leads**: Score 10-14 (Low priority, nurture campaign)
- **Cold Leads**: Score <10 (No immediate action, archive)

### Phase 3: Discovery & Needs Assessment

#### 3.1 Discovery Call Preparation
**Time Allocation**: 30 minutes preparation per call
**Tools**: Calendar, Video Conferencing, Note-taking system

**Preparation Checklist**:
```markdown
## Discovery Call Preparation Checklist
### Research Completed
- [ ] Company website and business model understood
- [ ] Industry and competitive landscape researched
- [ ] Social media presence reviewed
- [ ] Key decision makers identified
- [ ] Previous technical projects reviewed (if any)

### Questions Prepared
- [ ] Business objectives and goals questions
- [ ] Technical requirements questions
- [ ] Budget and timeline questions
- [ ] Decision-making process questions
- [ ] Success criteria questions

### Materials Ready
- [ ] Screen sharing setup
- [ ] Note-taking system prepared
- [ ] Proposal template customized
- [ ] Portfolio examples selected
- [ ] Technical architecture draft prepared
```

#### 3.2 Discovery Call Execution
**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Format**: Video call with screen sharing capability

**Call Structure**:
```markdown
## Discovery Call Agenda (30-45 minutes)

### Introduction (5 minutes)
- Welcome and introductions
- Understanding of their project context
- Agenda overview
- Expected outcomes

### Business Discovery (15 minutes)
- **Business Goals**: What are you trying to achieve?
- **Problem Statement**: What specific problems are you solving?
- **Success Metrics**: How will you measure success?
- **Target Users**: Who will use this solution?
- **Business Impact**: What ROI are you expecting?

### Technical Discovery (10 minutes)
- **Current Systems**: What technology are you currently using?
- **Technical Requirements**: Platforms, integrations, constraints
- **Scalability Needs**: Expected user growth and usage
- **Security Requirements**: Data protection and compliance needs

### Project Logistics (10 minutes)
- **Timeline**: When do you need this completed?
- **Budget**: What budget range have you allocated?
- **Decision Process**: Who makes the final decision?
- **Key Stakeholders**: Who else needs to be involved?

### Next Steps (5 minutes)
- Recap key points
- Explain proposal process
- Confirm timeline for deliverables
- Schedule follow-up
```

### Phase 5: Negotiation & Agreement

#### 5.1 Negotiation Handling
**Principles**:
- Focus on value, not just price
- Be prepared to offer options
- Understand client's constraints
- Maintain professional relationship
- Know your walk-away point

**Common Negotiation Scenarios**:
```markdown
## Negotiation Scenarios and Responses

### Budget Constraints
**Client**: "Your proposal is over our budget."
**Response Options**:
1. **Reduce Scope**: "Let's look at removing [specific features] to meet your budget"
2. **Phase Approach**: "We can implement Phase 1 now within budget and Phase 2 later"
3. **Alternative Solutions**: "Here's a different approach that could work within your budget"

### Timeline Pressure
**Client**: "We need this done faster than your timeline."
**Response Options**:
1. **Accelerated Timeline**: "I can accelerate the timeline by bringing in contractors at additional cost"
2. **MVP Approach**: "We can launch with core features sooner and add features later"
3. **Resource Allocation**: "I can dedicate more hours to your project if you can adjust the timeline for other projects"

### Scope Changes
**Client**: "We need additional features not in the original scope."
**Response Options**:
1. **Change Order**: "I can provide a quote for these additional features as a separate project"
2. **Trade-offs**: "We can add these features but need to remove other features or extend the timeline"
3. **Prioritization**: "Let's focus on the most critical features and move others to Phase 2"
```

#### 5.3 Agreement Process
**Steps**:
1. **Contract Drafting** (1 day after proposal acceptance)
2. **Contract Review** (2-3 days for client review)
3. **Negotiation of Terms** (As needed)
4. **Contract Signing** (Electronically or physically)
5. **Deposit Payment** (30% upon signing)
6. **Project Kickoff** (Within 3 days of deposit)

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Issue: Low Lead Quality
**Solutions**:
- Improve targeting in marketing content
- Better qualification criteria
- Focus on specific industries
- Improve call-to-action clarity

### Issue: Long Sales Cycle
**Solutions**:
- Better qualification upfront
- Clearer value proposition
- Streamlined proposal process
- Faster follow-up procedures

### Issue: Price Negotiations
**Solutions**:
- Clear value demonstration
- Multiple pricing options
- ROI calculations
- Payment term flexibility

### Issue: Communication Delays
**Solutions**:
- Clear communication protocols
- Regular follow-up schedule
- Multiple contact methods
- Response time commitments

## Best Practices

1. **Personalize all communications** - Reference specific client needs and goals
2. **Respond quickly** - Within 24 hours for all inquiries
3. **Document everything** - Keep detailed records of all interactions
4. **Build relationships** - Focus on long-term partnerships
5. **Be prepared** - Research clients before meetings
6. **Follow up consistently** - Regular, value-added follow-ups
7. **Know your limits** - Don't overpromise or take on too much
8. **Maintain professionalism** - Even when deals don't materialize

This SOP provides a systematic approach to client acquisition that ensures consistency, professionalism, and effectiveness in building a sustainable solo developer business.
